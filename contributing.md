# Contributing to This Site

How to update, improve, and maintain this Product Practice documentation. This is a living resource that evolves with our practice.

---

## Why Contribute?

This site works best when it reflects our current reality and incorporates lessons we learn. Your contributions ensure the documentation stays relevant, accurate, and useful for everyone.

**When You Should Contribute:**
- You discover outdated information
- You identify a gap or missing resource
- You develop a new template or process
- You learn something worth sharing
- You see an opportunity to improve clarity

---

## What You Can Contribute

### Content Updates

**Corrections:**
- Fix outdated information
- Correct inaccuracies
- Update links and references
- Improve clarity or remove ambiguity

**Enhancements:**
- Add examples and case studies
- Include diagrams or visuals
- Expand explanations
- Add FAQ sections

**New Content:**
- Document new processes
- Share lessons learned
- Add templates or tools
- Create how-to guides

---

### Structural Improvements

**Navigation:**
- Improve page organization
- Add missing cross-references
- Create clearer pathways to information
- Enhance search and discoverability

**Formatting:**
- Improve readability
- Add or improve tables/lists
- Use consistent styling
- Include helpful callouts

---

## Contribution Process

### Small Updates (Corrections, Minor Edits)

**Process:**
1. Identify the issue or improvement
2. Submit change request to Product Practice Lead
3. Review and approval within 48 hours
4. Update made and team notified

**Submission Format:**
- **Page:** [Page name or URL]
- **Issue:** [What needs to change]
- **Proposed Fix:** [Suggested update]
- **Why:** [Brief rationale]

---

### Medium Updates (New Sections, Major Rewrites)

**Process:**
1. Draft proposed content
2. Share draft with Product Strategist
3. Collaborative review and refinement
4. Approval from Product Practice Lead
5. Update made and communicated
6. Monitor for feedback

**Submission Format:**
- **Scope:** [What you're adding or changing]
- **Rationale:** [Why this is needed]
- **Draft:** [Proposed content]
- **Impact:** [Who benefits, how used]

---

### Large Updates (New Pages, Structural Changes)

**Process:**
1. Proposal to Product Practice Lead
2. Alignment on approach and scope
3. Collaborative development
4. Review with relevant stakeholders
5. Executive approval (if strategic)
6. Implementation and rollout
7. Team training if needed

**Proposal Format:**
- **Objective:** [What you want to achieve]
- **Rationale:** [Why this is important]
- **Scope:** [What will change]
- **Stakeholders:** [Who needs to review]
- **Timeline:** [How long will it take]
- **Resources:** [What you need]

## Content Guidelines

### Writing Style

**Be Clear and Concise:**
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid jargon unless necessary
- Define terms when first used
- Use active voice

**Be Practical:**
- Focus on application, not theory
- Include examples and scenarios
- Provide actionable guidance
- Link to resources and templates

**Be Positive:**
- Focus on what to do, not what to avoid
- Frame challenges as opportunities
- Use affirming, constructive language
- Avoid negative constructions

---

### Structure and Organization

**Consistent Formatting:**
- Use standard markdown formatting
- Follow existing page templates
- Include version and date footer
- Add navigation links

**Clear Hierarchy:**
- Use descriptive headers
- Maintain logical flow
- Group related content
- Use lists and tables appropriately

**Accessibility:**
- Write for diverse audiences
- Consider different experience levels
- Include context and background
- Link to related resources

---

### Quality Standards

**Accuracy:**
- Verify facts and figures
- Include sources when relevant
- Update regularly to maintain currency
- Remove or archive outdated content

**Completeness:**
- Address the full scope of topic
- Include all relevant information
- Link to additional resources
- Acknowledge gaps or limitations

**Usability:**
- Easy to scan and navigate
- Quick to find key information
- Clear next steps and actions
- Helpful examples and templates

## Review and Approval

### Approval Levels

**No Approval Needed:**
- Typo fixes
- Broken link repairs
- Formatting corrections
- Version/date updates

**Product Strategist Approval:**
- New templates or tools
- Process documentation
- Technical content
- Training materials

**Product Practice Lead Approval:**
- New pages or sections
- Strategic content
- Policy changes
- Major structural changes

**Executive Approval:**
- Product Principles changes
- Manifesto updates
- North Star metric changes
- Strategic direction shifts

## Version Control

### Versioning Approach

**Version Format:** Major.Minor (e.g., 1.0, 1.1, 2.0)

**Major Version (X.0):**
- Significant restructuring
- New sections or major capabilities
- Strategic direction changes

**Minor Version (X.Y):**
- Content updates and enhancements
- New templates or tools
- Process refinements

## Content Archival

### When to Archive

Content is archived (not deleted) when:
- Process is deprecated or replaced
- Information is no longer relevant
- Better alternative exists
- Consolidation improves organization

**Archive Location:** [Link to archive folder]

**Archive Format:**
- Original content preserved
- Archive date noted
- Reason for archival documented
- Replacement or alternative referenced

## Getting Started with Contributing

**Ready to Contribute?**

1. **Identify:** What needs to be added or improved?
2. **Draft:** Create your proposed content or changes
3. **Submit:** Send to appropriate reviewer (see approval levels)
4. **Collaborate:** Work with reviewers to refine
5. **Publish:** Content is added and team is notified
6. **Monitor:** Gather feedback and iterate

**Questions About Contributing?**
- Ask in #product-practice Slack channel
- Contact Product Strategist
- Discuss in 1:1 with Product Practice Lead
