# Onboarding Guide

How new team members get started in the Product Practice. This guide accelerates your path to productivity and impact.

## Welcome to the Product Practice

You're joining a team building AI-enabled workflows that deliver measurable economic impact. We design processes that clients can own and operate independently.

**Our mission:** Design, prove, and scale AI-enabled processes that deliver measurable economic impact.

**Our measure of success:** Workflows automated with verified dollars saved or earned.

## Onboarding Project

Every new team member completes an onboarding project within first 30 days.

**Project Options:**

**Option 1: Case Study Development**
- Select completed workflow
- Interview team members and client
- Document impact and lessons learned
- Present to Product Practice team

**Option 2: Process Improvement**
- Identify workflow development pain point
- Research best practices
- Propose improvement with implementation plan
- Present to Product Practice Lead

**Option 3: Template or Tool Creation**
- Identify gap in template library
- Develop new template or tool
- Test with team members
- Add to template library with documentation

**Deliverable Format:**
- 15-minute presentation to Product Practice team
- Documentation or artifact produced
- Reflection on learning

### Common New Team Member Questions

**Q: How do I prioritize when multiple workflows need attention?**
A: Discuss with Product Practice Lead. Generally: phase gates, client commitments, then strategic priorities.

**Q: What if a client asks for something outside our scope?**
A: Document the request, discuss with Account team and Product Practice Lead, assess against intake criteria.

**Q: How much autonomy should I have at each stage?**
A: Week 1-2: Shadow. Week 3-4: Contribute with oversight. Month 2: Manage tasks. Month 3: Own components.

**Q: What if I disagree with an approach or decision?**
A: Share your perspective respectfully. We value diverse viewpoints and constructive challenge.
