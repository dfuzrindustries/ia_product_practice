# Roles & Responsibilities

RACI matrix defining accountability and coordination across the Product Practice. Clear roles eliminate ambiguity and enable effective execution.

---

## RACI Framework

**R = Responsible** — Does the work  
**A = Accountable** — Owns the outcome and has decision authority  
**C = Consulted** — Provides input before decisions/actions  
**I = Informed** — Kept updated on progress and decisions

---

## Product Practice Core Roles

### Product Practice Lead
**Accountable for:** Product Practice strategy, performance, and economic impact

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Set Product Practice vision and roadmap
- Own North Star metrics and performance
- Manage Product Practice team and budget
- Lead Executive Business Reviews
- Approve major investment decisions
- Build partnerships and strategic relationships

---

### Product Manager
**Accountable for:** Individual workflow delivery and economic impact

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Manage workflow from POV through Program
- Design human-agent collaboration models
- Track and verify economic impact
- Coordinate capability transfer
- Ensure governance and measurement embedded
- Manage client relationships for assigned workflows

---

### Product Strategist
**Accountable for:** Workflow design patterns and best practices

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Develop reusable workflow templates
- Document lessons learned and patterns
- Support POV and workshop facilitation
- Maintain product playbooks and documentation
- Enable Product Working Group with training

---

### AI Solutions Architect
**Accountable for:** Technical feasibility and agent design

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Assess technical readiness
- Design agent capabilities and integration
- Validate technical approach in POC
- Support pilot deployment
- Document technical patterns

---

## Product Working Group Roles

### Account Team
**Accountable for:** Client relationship health and expansion

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Identify new workflow opportunities
- Coordinate POV workshops
- Manage client executive relationships
- Identify expansion and partnership opportunities
- Ensure client satisfaction

---

### Delivery Team
**Accountable for:** Workflow implementation and deployment

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Build and deploy workflows
- Execute POC and Pilot phases
- Provide technical expertise
- Support capability transfer
- Maintain production systems

---

### Sales Team
**Accountable for:** Pipeline generation and deal closure

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Qualify opportunities
- Coordinate Day 1 Workshops
- Close SOWs for POC, Pilot, Program
- Use case studies in sales process
- Provide market feedback

---

### Platform Team
**Accountable for:** Infrastructure and tooling

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Provide technical infrastructure
- Build reusable components and tools
- Support integration and deployment
- Maintain security and compliance
- Enable workflow scalability

## Decision Authority Levels

### Strategic Decisions (Product Practice Lead)
- Product Practice roadmap and priorities
- North Star metric targets
- Major investment allocations (>$100K)
- Partnership agreements
- Team structure and hiring

### Tactical Decisions (Product Manager)
- Workflow design choices
- POC/Pilot scope and timeline
- Resource allocation within workflow
- Go/No-Go recommendations
- Client engagement approach

### Operational Decisions (Delivery Team)
- Technical implementation details
- Tool and technology choices
- Deployment scheduling
- Issue resolution and troubleshooting

---

## Escalation Path

**Level 1:** Product Manager  
**Level 2:** Product Practice Lead  
**Level 3:** Executive Leadership Team

**Escalate when:**
- Phase gate decision is unclear or contested
- Client relationship at risk
- Economic impact below threshold
- Resource constraints blocking progress
- Strategic alignment needed

---

## Using This RACI

**Before Starting Work:**
- Check who is Accountable for the outcome
- Identify who needs to be Consulted
- Ensure Responsible parties are available
- Confirm who should be Informed

**When Issues Arise:**
- Contact the Accountable person first
- Escalate if decision authority is unclear
- Document decisions and rationale

**Regular Review:**
- RACI is reviewed quarterly
- Updated based on lessons learned
- Adjusted for new processes or roles
